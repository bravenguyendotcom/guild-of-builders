# 📓 BUILDERS_LOGBOOK.md — Your Voyage, Your Pace

### Progress, ranks, and pacing for the Guild of Builders

> **Status:** DRAFT.
> **North Star:** everything here exists to help a child *keep building* — never to rank one
> child above another. If a feature could shame a slower Builder, it isn't a Guild feature.

---

## The one rule of pace

**This is a race against yourself — not against other Builders.**

Finishing is the achievement. *When* you finish is not the point. A Builder who takes a year
and a Builder who takes a month arrive at the same place: *"I built this."*

The only scoreboard that matters:

> **Today's Builder vs. yesterday's Builder.**

---

## 🌫️ The real enemies — the Fog Creatures of Python Planet

The toughest boss in any adventure isn't a bug. It's the little voice that says *"later,"*
*"I can't,"* or *"it has to be perfect."* On Python Planet, those voices take shape as the
**Fog Creatures** — and they visit **every** Builder. Even Captain Byte. Even Dragon Debug,
once, long ago.

| The creature | What it whispers | Your weapon |
|--------------|------------------|-------------|
| 🌫️ **The Fog of "I Can't"** | "You're not smart enough." | Try **Version 1**. Just one line. |
| 🦑 **The Snooze Kraken** | "Do it tomorrow." | Open the file and take **one small step.** |
| ⏳ **The Quicksand of Later** | "Not right now..." | **Ship a donut today.** 🍩 |
| ✨ **The Gremlin of Never-Good-Enough** | "It's still not perfect." | *"Version 1 beats perfect."* |
| 📱 **The Siren of Distraction** | "Ooh, look over there!" | **One quest, one focus.** |

Beating them isn't about being faster than other kids. It's about becoming **a little braver
than yesterday's you.** That's the only race worth running — and every Builder can win it.

## 🧭 Choose your track (and switch anytime)

Different Builders sail differently. **You can be Fast in Volume I and Gentle in Volume IV.**
That's not just allowed — it's normal.

- 🏴‍☠️ **Fast Track (Explorer)** — you read and code quickly.
  *Your job: never be bored.* Do **every** Bonus Challenge ⭐, take the **Side-Quests**, and
  after each volume, **build something of your own** before moving on. Turn spare speed into
  more building — not into waiting.
- ⛵ **Standard Track (Navigator)** — one chapter and its mission per sitting. Steady and sure.
- 🌱 **Gentle Track (Steady)** — shorter sessions, re-read when you like, use read-aloud, take
  all the time you want. Perfect for younger Builders, or anyone who likes to *savor* the voyage.

> Switching down when a volume gets hard isn't quitting — it's smart sailing.
> Switching up when you're flying isn't showing off — it's staying awake.

## 🗺️ The pacing map (a map, not a deadline)

Rough time to sail the **whole series** (all four volumes), at a regular, relaxed cadence:

| Age group | Typical whole-series time |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **8–11** | ~6–10 months |
| **12–15** | ~3–6 months |
| **16+** | ~2–4 months (as little as ~6 weeks if focused & already comfy with code) |

**How to read this map:**
- It assumes roughly **2–4 hours a week**, fairly regularly. More hours → faster. Life happens → slower. Both are fine.
- **Volume I flies.** Some Builders finish several chapters in an hour (Tommy did!).
- **Volumes III–IV are heavier** — real graphics, then AI. Expect each chapter to take longer,
  and expect your pace to slow down. **That's correct, not failure.**
- Rough effort split, so nothing surprises you: **Vol I ~20% · Vol II ~25% · Vol III ~30% · Vol IV ~25%.**

If a Builder ever feels *behind*, they are not. There is no behind. There is only *next step.*

## 🎯 Series Definition of Done (the scope & the output)

A project needs a finish line. Here is ours. You have **finished the Guild of Builders** when
you can honestly say all three:

1. **I shipped four generations of one real game** — CLI → bigger → published → AI-powered.
2. **I earned the badges and reached 🏛️ Guild Builder.**
3. **I can take a brand-new idea and know how to start building it.**

That's the scope. That's the deliverable. Everything past it is bonus adventure. 🍩

## 🌅 Where this voyage leads

When the map runs out, the *real* voyage begins. A Guild Builder is ready to:

- build something **with a grown-up they love** (maybe even Dad 😉),
- ship their own small **indie game**,
- make a tool that helps a friend, a class, or a family,
- or keep sailing into bigger, real projects.

The series doesn't end by closing a book. It ends by **handing the child the pen.**

---

## 👪 For parents

- **Celebrate effort and shipping — never speed.** The child who struggles and ships has won.
- **Let a fast Builder run ahead** into side-quests and their own projects. For them, *boredom*
  is the real enemy — don't hold them at a slow pace.
- **Let a steady Builder take all the time they need.** The finish line never moves.
- The only comparison worth making out loud: *"Look how much braver you are than last month."*

⚓ *Your voyage. Your pace. The only Builder you're racing is the one you were yesterday.*
